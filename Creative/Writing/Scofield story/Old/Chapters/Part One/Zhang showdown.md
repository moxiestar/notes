## **Adela fights with Leo, who lies about what happened with Diana.**

When Leo opens the door, his sister is standing in the hall. 
“Oh my God,” she says, her eyes darting across his face. “Holy shit.”
“Shh.” Leo pulls her inside and locks the door behind her.
“Ma’s out, she went next door for Mahjongg. Dude, what the fuck happened? Where’s Diana?”
Leo shakes his head. “I don’t know.”
Adela hesitates. “What do you mean, you don’t know? You don’t know what happened, or you don’t know where she is?”
Her brother doesn’t answer.
“Or both?”
“Both.”
“Fuck,” Adela says. “I leave you alone for one hour —”
“It wasn’t me! It was her. Adela, she isn’t who she says she is.”
His sister stops. “What do you mean?”
“I think she’s kind of… I don’t think she’s…” He frowns. “She’s crazy. She’s like a science experiment or something. She’s a… she’s a monster. She did this.” He gestures to his face.
Adela rolls her eyes. “Look, I know you don’t like her, but come on. She’s not a monster. She’s scared. She’s scared of everything. And she doesn’t know anything. You’ve heard how she talks. There’s no way in hell she could’ve done something like that. It looks like you slit your cheek open with a razor. She didn’t do that.”
“Adela, I’m telling you, she did this. And the window. I was with her in your room, and —”
“What the hell were you doing in my room?”
“*That’s* what you’re upset about?” Leo gives her a look. “Anyway, she… she freaked out, and I tried to get her to calm down, but I guess I just made it worse, and all of a sudden she was gone. And now the window’s open and — and my face is messed up.”
“What do you mean, all of a sudden she was gone?”
Leo groans. “I don’t know! She was there one second, and the next she wasn’t. She’s got, like, magic powers or something. She’s nuts, Adela. She’s not human.” He hesitates. “I think it’s good that she’s gone.”
Adela leans back against the bathroom door and sighs. She walks slowly over to the toilet, brushing past her brother, and sits down on the lid. “Fuck,” she says, putting her head in her hands. Her bangs splay back from her hairline and fan through her fingers. There’s a big, long silence, the kind of silence that boils up right before a bomb goes off. “Do we call the cops?” 
“No, we’d just get in more trouble if we did.” Leo shakes his head. “We’d be — what’s the word — implicating ourselves. And Ma would find out.”
“Fuck,” Adela repeats, drawing it out. 
“Watch your language.”
Adela mimes punching a hole in the wall. “I swear to God, you are just like Ma sometimes,” she mutters. “What’s gonna happen to Diana out there? She’s not gonna make it on her own. And maybe they’ll catch her. Someone’s responsible for this. Someone did that to her.”
“Who cares? She’s gone now. We can’t get wrapped up in this.”
“What the fuck do you mean, who cares? She’s my friend, Leo. I care. She’s hurt and she’s scared and she has fucking no one out there.”
“What if she was lying about everything?” Leo says.
Adela shakes her head. “I just don’t think she would do that. And I don’t buy that she lashed out at you. I mean, I guess she could’ve, but I don’t think she would’ve unless she was really freaked out.”
Leo makes a face.
“Is there something you’re not telling me?” Adela narrows her eyes. “What were you doing in my room?”
“Nothing,” Leo says, but his voice has gotten higher.
“Do not fucking bullshit me about this.”
“It’s none of your business.”
“It’s *my room.*” Adela steps in. “I know you think you’re top man around here just because Ma likes you better. But you cannot fucking lie to me about this.”
“I…” Leo looks down and groans. “Look, we got carried away, okay? She was acting super weird, and she was saying all this stuff about how she was into me. And then she kissed me.”
Adela scoffs. Then she gets quiet. “Are you kidding?”
“Why would I kid about this?”
His sister covers her face with her hands. “No, Leo, she wouldn’t do that. I know she wouldn’t do that. You’re bullshitting me. She probably doesn’t even know what kissing is. What did you do?”
Leo throws up his hands. “I didn’t do anything!”
“What was she saying to you?”
“Just all this stuff about how she liked me. How she thought I was cute. I don’t know, Adela. It was messed up. And I thought I would go along with it because she’s so naïve, you know. I didn’t want to burst her bubble or whatever. And now Lila’s gonna find out, and —”
“Yeah, I hope she does. You cheated on her. Doesn’t matter who started it, you kissed another girl.”
“Diana’s not a *girl*. I’m not even convinced she’s a person.”
“Okay, you are the problem right now,” Adela says. “Do you fucking hear yourself?”
“Look, just leave my girlfriend out of this.”
“Fuck leaving her out of this. You brought her into this. This is your issue, not mine. I’m not the one cheating.”
Leo rolls his eyes. “You’re just acting butt hurt ’cause you hate Lila.”
“This is not about me, Leo. This is about you and it’s about Diana.”
“Right, so we shouldn’t be talking about Lila. But you just had to bring her up.”
“She’s not exactly Joan of fucking Arc. I mean, come on, who the fuck calls their boyfriend’s sister a chink? Doesn’t that bother you even a little bit? I know it does, cause you’ve got this puppy-ass look on your face, so don’t even try lying to me.”
“Okay, yeah, it was out of line, sure, but...” Leo hesitates.
Adela scoffs. “Out of line?”
“Sometimes she says crazy stuff. She didn’t mean it. But you called her a dumb blonde, and I know you, and I know you meant it.”
“Oh, really?” Adela says. “Someday, she’s gonna call you a chink nerd, and then we’ll see whether she means it or not. You are so fucking oblivious, Leo.”
“I am not fucking oblivious.”
“Yes, you are.” Adela gets up. “You are so oblivious that — here’s how oblivious you are. You think —”
“I don’t *think* anything. I’m looking out for you, Adela. I’m always looking out for you.”
“No, you fucking aren’t! You’re trying to control me. And it sucks, Leo. Why won’t you just talk to me and stop acting like you’re so superior? You’re in on this too, you know. You let her stay here. You helped me hide her. You fucking kissed her.”
Leo screws up his face. “Okay, you know what? You’re done.” He opens the bathroom door, slamming it behind him. “You’re done,” he shouts, his muffled voice echoing in the hall.
“Bitch,” Adela yells after him. “Fucking bitch.”
