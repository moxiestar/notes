## **The surgical team tries to save a young boy.**

The last case went long. 
Scofield can’t remember what happened, but he has a feeling that something went wrong. The bipolar is shaking in his hand. He’s standing above the operating table, peering into the hole in his tiny skull. Through the drapes, he can see the patient’s silhouette: the body of a young boy. But he can’t remember who the boy is. His name is probably in that stack of files on his desk, buried two or three down. He must have stopped in his office before starting the operation. There was something he had to do. 
“Suction,” Scofield says, nodding to the tech. 
“Yes, sir.” Her eyes look like Bonnie’s, but her voice is off. Scofield can’t place it. 
He glances down at the burr hole, flushes it with saline. He reaches for the bipolar from his nurse. “We don’t have all day here,” he mutters. When he looks down again, his gloves are covered in blood. It’s pouring from the boy’s skull. 
“Hold on.” He waves to Bonnie. “Suction.”
“I am suctioning.”
“Shit. Something’s…”
Bonnie steps closer. “Where’s it coming from?”
“No idea. Get me a towel.”
But the towels are already on the floor. He watches as the blood seeps through them, the red stain growing larger and larger. It’s dripping off the drapes onto the floor. It’s running down his gown. 
“Shit! He’s hemorrhaging.”
In the background, one of the monitors is sounding an alarm. “BP’s in the tank, Nick,” Taylor calls. 
“Okay,” Scofield says. “Okay. We need to try to find the…” He can’t get the words out. The blood is pooling around his feet, soaking his shoelaces. “This isn’t right.” Somehow, the surgical drapes have begun to slip out of place. He can see the boy’s feet, that cadaver-pale skin, at the end of the table. 
The nurses are backing away. In the corner, Bonnie’s talking to the circulator. “… and fast, too. Who’s the trauma surgeon on call?”
“Carson,” Scofield says, shaking his head. “He can’t fix this.”
Taylor’s rushing over. “He’s coding, Nick. You need to stop the bleeding.”
“I see that, I see it. Taylor, start compressions. Emma, get a crash cart in here. Don’t just look at me, move! Will someone please get me the fucking towels?”
Emma frowns. “There are no towels. We’ve run out.”
“Oh, for fuck’s sake,” Scofield snaps. “He’s not gonna make it.” Again he falters. There’s something stopping him from speaking, something in the back of his throat. “He’s not… Fuck. Intubate.”
“We don’t have oxygen.”
“Go get it then. This is a hospital, you idiot. We aren’t in the middle of the Sahara here. Come on, now.” Scofield’s wringing his hands. When he lowers them, reaching for the gauze, he feels warmth at his fingertips. Blood, too much blood. It’s filling the operating room, knee deep and sticky like molasses. The monitor gets louder, then quieter again, as if it’s circling him. He’s lost the gauze, dropped it in the sea of blood. He presses his hands up against the boy’s skull, and the blood pours out between his fingers. 
“Shit!” He tries to breathe in, but can’t. “Shit. One of you, get over here.”
“Dr. Scofield…”
“Get over here.”
“Dr. Scofield. Dr. Scofield.”
Why can’t he breathe? 
“Dr. Scofield…”
