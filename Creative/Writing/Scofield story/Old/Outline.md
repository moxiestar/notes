## *Blow by blow:

**Scofield is introduced.**
Curtis leaves the hospital. 
Scofield fights with Ann. 

**Diana finds Leo and Adela.**

Scofield has a nightmare.
Eleanor comes over, asking if he will mentor Tony. 

At school, Tony asks Adela if they can be partners on the science project. Adela agrees. Leo’s girlfriend Lila bullies Adela, and Mr. Tsao, the science teacher, stands up for Adela when she’s threatened with detention. Mr. Tsao gives a lecture on robots.

Scofield backs into McLaughlin’s mailbox. They fight. Scofield leaves for the OR. 

Diana explores the Zhang house. 
Leo comes home early, and he and Diana talk. 

The next morning, Scofield goes to McLaughlin’s house to apologize, but McLaughlin is pissed. 
That afternoon, Scofield sees Tony. 

Scofield has another nightmare. 
Tony calls Adela at Plastic Fantastic to discuss the science project.
At home, Leo and Adela talk to Diana. She opens up, revealing that her mother created her to prove her medical abilities to her father (Scofield). 

Eleanor and Scofield go out to dinner.

Adela stays after school and talks to Mr. Tsao. He suggests the MP3 player. Back home, Mrs Zhang yells at Leo and nearly discovers Diana, making him nervous about keeping her. **Later, he kisses her, but she gets scared and runs away.** 

Scofield has another nightmare.

When Adela gets home, Leo lies to her, telling her that Diana instigated the kiss. Adela does not believe him. 

==**Scofield tries commit suicide by overdosing on diazepam, but regrets it at the last minute and manages to administer an antidote.** He wakes, unsure if it was a dream. Later, Eleanor visits, and they have sex. The next morning, he makes her breakfast.

At school, Adela talks to Mr. Tsao. She tells him vague details about Diana and admits she’s worried she’ll never have a real friend. Mr. Tsao comforts her, subtly revealing that he is aware of Diana and even perhaps of the government research. Tony happens to walk by, and takes her out roller skating. He asks her out, but she politely turns him down. 

**While out on patrol, McLaughlin comes across Diana, and brings her into the police station. He manages to get some information out of her, and opens an investigation into Christina Curtis. When his shift ends and he tries to go home, Diana begs him not to leave her alone in the holding cell. He stays the night with her, bringing her a blanket and a stuffed animal from the archives.** 
Meanwhile, Curtis shows up at Eleanor’s house, and appears like a kind and innocent mother who is desperately searching for her only child. Eleanor says that she hasn’t heard anything about a missing child and wishes Curtis luck. When Curtis leaves, Eleanor calls McLaughlin and tells him Curtis’ behavior is suspicious.
The next morning, McLaughlin calls Scofield to come down to the station, hoping he can identify her neural issues. Scofield recognizes that Diana was created using his implant research. McLaughlin cannot share more details about the case with Scofield and does not admit that he suspects Curtis. 
Later, on a tip from Eleanor, McLaughlin stops by the record store to ask Adela if she knows Diana. When she confirms that she does, he interviews her before allowing her to briefly reunite with Diana. The two girls share a hug and Adela lends her some books from the school library. Diana is obviously relieved to see Adela, whom she is beginning to view as a sister. 
**That night, McLaughlin, having received a tip about the location of the lab, goes alone to explore. He discovers that Curtis uses cadavers and prisoners to create her cyborgs, and he is disgusted by her work. Two FBI agents show up as he tries to leave, but he shoots and kills them both. When he returns to the station, Diana is gone. At his house, Scofield gets a call from Curtis.** 

After school, Adela spends the afternoon with Eleanor and Tony, where they play Resident Evil 2 and eat dinner. Tony shows off his MP3 player design. Adela tells him about Diana, and Tony is fascinated by the idea of meeting a cyborg.

**At the station, McLaughlin argues with his supervisor, who insists that he stand down from the FBI, who have forbidden the police from inquiring into Diana’s case.** Adela returns to check on Diana, this time with Tony tagging along. Turning off the cameras in the interrogation room, McLaughlin interviews them both, then promises to help them find Diana. 

McLaughlin visits Scofield to catch him up on the situation. Both are becoming paranoid and distrustful, and McLaughlin insists they put on loud music before talking, afraid that the FBI could be listening. McLaughlin tells Scofield that Diana is likely Curtis’ daughter, and, by extension, likely Scofield’s daughter too. Scofield is disturbed. He asks McLaughlin what he found at the lab, but McLaughlin is at a loss for words. Scofield tells him that Curtis called him recently, but only said hello before hanging up. 

Tony and Adela are back at Eleanor’s house. She is a little dubious, but trusts her nephew and likes Adela, so she listens. Scofield and McLaughlin stop by, revealing their involvement, and the five of them discuss a plan. Adela and McLaughlin begin to connect over their shared rebelliousness, while Eleanor encourages Scofield to contact Curtis and try to set up a trap to retrieve Diana. He dials her on his flip phone while the others try to listen to the call. 

Back with Leo, who has seen the error of his ways. He is trying to write a text to Adela, who, he hopes, will forgive him. Hearing his mother put on traditional Chinese music downstairs, he remembers the last time he saw his father alive, and relives the plane crash that caused his father’s death. Looking under his bed, he finds what appears to be a tracking device, and activates it. 

Curtis agrees to meet Scofield alone. McLaughlin tapes him so he can hear their conversation. Curtis admits that Diana is Scofield’s daughter, but tells him that Diana is sick, and must be kept safe in the lab. She tries to convince him to join her at the lab, but he refuses. She confirms that the implant she used to create Diana was based on Scofield’s designs, telling him that she succeeded where he failed. He leaves, disgusted. As he is leaving, Curtis receives a call from one of her administrators, revealing that the tracking device was linked to Diana. Believing Diana is at the Zhangs’ house, Curtis and the FBI arrive with guns. 

McLaughlin, now equally invested, decides he will arm the three of them — himself, Scofield, and Eleanor — to break into the lab. After arriving and overpowering security, mostly thanks to McLaughlin, they find the lab deserted. Scofield reunites with Diana. 
*Meanwhile, back at Eleanor’s house, Adela and Tony are waiting for the adults to return. Adela gets a panicked call from her brother, but the connection is suddenly cut off. Adela takes Scofield’s prize Ferrari, totaling it along the way, and drives them to the Zhangs’ house, where the kids find Mrs. Zhang and Leo dead. Curtis and the FBI agents take the kids hostage, and drive back to the lab.* 
At the lab, McLaughlin, Scofield, Diana, and Eleanor prepare to leave, but Curtis corners them, telling them they have fallen into her trap. She reveals that she has the kids at gunpoint, and will only let them go if they abandon Diana. Diana suddenly collapses, and Scofield bargains a deal with Curtis: together, they will perform a final operation on Diana, hopefully saving her life. As Diana passes in and out of consciousness, Scofield gives her a choice, and Diana chooses to let him do the operation. Curtis and Scofield, with the help of Eleanor, perform an emergency surgery to save Diana’s life. 
After the surgery is over, Curtis takes Scofield into the storeroom while Eleanor monitors Diana’s vitals. When Scofield returns alone, Eleanor learns the Curtis committed suicide. 
Outside, McLaughlin manages to overpower the guards, then breaks in to the operating room. 
The group tries to leave in Scofield’s car, and Adela comes clean about the accident. They plan to take McLaughlin’s Ford, but are stopped by Erhart and Singleterry, who want Scofield to give up Diana. Erhart and Singleterry take Scofield and Diana aside and broker a deal. All things seemingly settled, the men escort the group away from the lab. 
With nowhere else to go, Adela goes home with McLaughlin. He comforts her through her grief over the loss of her family and nearly her best friend. Together, they watch an episode of the X Files.
Meanwhile, Eleanor and Tony stay with Scofield and Diana. Tony and Diana become fast friends, with Tony telling her about the science project and Diana showing off her bionic limbs. When Diana has a nightmare, Scofield reassures her that she is okay. She describes seeing Henry in her dream, and Scofield is moved. She asks if Scofield is her father, and he admits that he is. He sleeps in a chair in the corner of her room so she feels safe. Before she falls asleep, she tells him that she knew he would always come back to her, paralleling Curtis’ last promise to Scofield. 

In an epilogue sequence, Scofield resigns from Duke and takes up a similar position at UNC. He has a nightmare that Curtis has been experimenting on his son’s corpse. Tired, he leaves work early to see Diana and prepare for his New Year’s party. 
McLaughlin and Adela arrive before the other guests, and Adela goes upstairs to help Diana with her dress. McLaughlin tells Scofield that he has been looking after Adela and plans to adopt her. Scofield asks if McLaughlin ever came over to his house, and McLaughlin says that he did not. McLaughlin asks if Scofield killed Curtis, and Scofield gives a cryptic reply. 
Later, the others arrive: Eleanor and Tony bring video games and cookies, then Taylor and Lila (who tries to apologize to Adela), along with Scofield’s other colleagues. McLaughlin has invited part of the police department, too. Mr. Tsao arrives last, and McLaughlin appears to recognize him. 
The group watches the countdown from 1999 to 2000. 
On the porch, McLaughlin talks to Mr. Tsao, who tells him that the FBI is after them, and they must be careful. He warns that Scofield is not who he says he is. Diana joins McLaughlin in the kitchen and tells him “they’re always going to be watching.”
Scofield goes up to his room, where he reads a note from Curtis, promising the lab to him and leaving him the key. 

## *Sequel ideas:*

**Old idea:**
Scofield has continued to work at the hospital, rising up the ranks again and teaching more students. McLaughlin’s heart condition is progressing, with the doctors giving him a year to live. Diana, under a human guise, has become an F1 driver for McLaren. Adela has achieved her dreams of becoming an astronaut, and is engaged to Tony, who works at Boeing as a young engineer. Eleanor has been promoted, now working at UNC, where she teaches English to the creative writing cohort. Mr. Stripes has sadly passed away. Leo has returned to China with his mother, leaving Adela without family in the US. During an incident at the track, Diana is injured, revealing her identity as a cyborg. She is temporarily banned from F1. She stays with Tony and Adela, who help her petition the FIA to get the ban overturned, making F1 history. When McLaughlin passes away, Scofield kills himself by inducing a fatal accident in his Ferrari on the highway. Diana is the one who has to pull the plug, which she reluctantly does. After her first successful race back, she calls Leo to try to reconnect. 

**Future plans for the characters:**
- Scofield moves to functional neurosurgery at UNC, and is eventually promoted to chief. He takes on teaching roles in the hospital again and mentors several cohorts of residents. As an older man, he gets into a car crash in his beloved Ferrari and falls into a coma. Diana pulls the plug, with Allison and Eleanor saying goodbye. 
- McLaughlin becomes the lead detective in the homicide department and solves a cold case from the 80s. He becomes extremely close with Adela and devotes his life to raising her. Eventually, he dies of a heart attack.
- Eleanor stays at UNC, where she becomes top nurse in the ER. She has a close call with Tony’s parents, and ends up getting involved with the police and government, but manages to keep Tony. She and Scofield remain together till Scofield’s death, but never marry. She dies of lung cancer with Tony at her side. 
- Tony attends MIT for undergrad, where he double-majors in robotics and engineering. After meeting Beatrice at the science fair, they start dating in junior year of high school. He has to fly down the coast once a month to see Adela, Diana, and Beatrice - he works at a video game store to save up. As an adult, he becomes a mechanical engineer at Boston Dynamics. He and Beatrice get married and have one child, a girl named Ann.
- Adela goes to Duke (and rooms with Diana all four years). She studies computer science with a minor in music. She becomes the president of the skate club and founds a tech company with several of her classmates. In her free time, she works at the student radio. McLaughlin teaches her how to ride and eventually passes his Harley on to her. As an adult, after getting her masters in astrophysics, she becomes the first Chinese-American astronaut to fly with NASA. She also starts an initiative to get more girls into STEM.
- Diana also goes to Duke. She majors in biomedical engineering and volunteers at the hospital. In her spare time, she works as a mechanic and races Scofield’s cars (against his better judgement). She continues at Duke for medical school, where she eventually becomes a pediatric surgeon. She and Adela get engaged in college (Adela proposes to her with a custom ring that she built at the university maker space), but have to wait until gay marriage is legalized to have their wedding. They adopt several abandoned children whom Diana treats at the hospital.
- Lila goes to UNC and gets a B.A. in psychology. She rushes tri-delta, and becomes the president during undergrad. Later, she goes to Harvard Law and becomes a criminal justice lawyer specializing in domestic violence. On the side, she works as a columnist for a style magazine.
