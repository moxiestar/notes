# **Outline**

Early 2000s, Pacific Northwest. Diana Kowalski, a young detective at a small-town police station, is going through her ex’s stuff after their long-term relationship has fallen apart. A successful businessman, he has cut her off and moved to the city, leaving her to rebuild her life alone. Her high school friend, Katherine, encourages her to date around town, but Diana is uninterested.

Bill Scofield, once a successful psychiatrist, has relocated to the same small town after his life in the city fell apart. His wife Allison and daughter Sara, killed years ago in a drunk driving accident where the perpetrator got away, haunt his dreams and memories. Scofield now lives alone and works as a forensic psychiatrist at the police station, where he is unpopular and largely ignored. He refuses to go to a therapist, disavowing psychology as a field, and with no other way to cope, he turns to alcohol.

A teenage girl, Olivia Murphy, gets booked at the station. After a confusing initial statement, Scofield is brought in to conduct a psychological interview, which only raises more questions about her past and her wellbeing. He joins Diana, the main detective on the case, and they slowly start to get along. Over time, they begin having secret one-night-stands. Conflicting evidence about Murph begins to pile up, and both Scofield and Diana have the feeling that they’re being watched.

Struggling with depression, Diana begins to distance herself from the people her life. When she runs into her ex’s family around town, they make snarky comments about him being better off without her. She talks to the police captain at the station late at night, revealing that her father abandoned her as a child and her mother, an addict, has been in a facility since she was a teenager. She tells the police captain that her ex and his family made comments about her turning out like her mother, something that scares her more and more as she gets older. He insists that she is headed for something better, but she says her life has only gotten worse. While driving home past the pier, she has a vision of a sea creature in the water. When Scofield comes to work intoxicated the next day, Diana yells at him in front of everyone at the station, leading to warnings for them both.

At home later that night, Diana realizes she left her flip phone at the station. When she returns to the station to pick it up, she hears strange noises from Murph’s holding cell.

…

As Murph continues to get in trouble, Scofield starts to take her under his wing. When Murph is taken into custody after her parents are exposed as violent criminals, Scofield agrees to take her in. They bond over their former lives. After Murph tells Scofield about the abuse she survived at the hands of her family, Scofield hunts down and kills her relatives in revenge. He is caught by Diana, who wants to book him for his crimes but doesn’t have the guts. He tells her about his marriage, and promises to work on his problems, but isn’t sure how he’ll figure it out.

Back home, Murph runs away and is officially declared missing. Scofield and Diana mount a search. When they find her, Murph is fraught and upset, confessing that she isn’t sure she can keep living. Scofield takes her back home, and he and Diana care for her.

At the end of their first year together, Scofield adopts Murph. They share an ice cream in the clinic parking lot and look forward to their future.
