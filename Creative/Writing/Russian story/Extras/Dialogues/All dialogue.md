“No, Your Excellency,” Sasha said, wondering what counted as relations.

“Yes, Your Excellency. I wouldn’t bring disgrace on the school, or on myself.”

“You’ve nearly brought disgrace on both the school and yourself.”

“We didn’t do anything really wrong.”

“Oh, and you know all about right and wrong?”

A cab, closed and furtive, presented all kinds of dangerous possibilities. “I’d rather walk.”

“The uniform really suits you,” she said. “You look almost like a real officer.”

“I’m touched. How’s Father?”

“Could be worse.”

“And Mother?”

Rolling her eyes, Katya put on her best, teariest expression and clung on to his arm. “Oh Kitty dear,” she imitated, “you know you really shouldn’t go out on the main street dressed like that. “How’s Leningrad?”

“Later.”
