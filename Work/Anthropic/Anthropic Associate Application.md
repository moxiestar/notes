**Tell us about the most interesting AI project you've built.**

Over the past semester, I developed Mosaic ([mosaiclearn.dev](http://mosaiclearn.dev)), an AI development literacy program that teaches students to build apps with Claude and Claude Code. Mosaic guides learners through prompting etiquette, terminal usage, and computing fundamentals, providing a safe and creative foundation for development. Each level ends with a tangible artifact, whether that be a CSV, a Python script, or a full website. For me, Mosaic is the resource I never had, pushing young, curious minds to innovate and create with AI.

**Why do you want to work at Anthropic? (We value this response highly - great answers are often 200-400 words.)**

As an artist, my interest in AI has been fueled by a drive for creativity. My path into AI safety began in my sophomore year at UNC, combining cognitive neuroscience, empirical research, and hands-on work with large language models. Most recently, my work as a research assistant in the Hopfinger Lab at UNC sparked a passion for ethical AI. As part of my research, I spent a year creating a complex Python-based behavioral experiment to study attention and working memory, a project that directly shaped my understanding of how neural networks, both biological and artificial, represent and prioritize information. It forced me to think carefully about how models interact with experimental design in unexpected ways, making the questions at the heart of AI safety feel urgent and personal.

The Applied AI Associate program appeals to me because it’s structured around exactly the kind of empirical, output-driven research I want to be doing. During my time at UNC, I’ve been privileged enough to work in a number of different environments, gaining experience in research, entrepreneurship, and tech. Working as an Associate at Anthropic would allow me to combine and build on my knowledge across three domains, with mentorship from developers who are defining the field. I’m hopeful that my background in cognitive psychology and neuroscience provides a perspective that’s less common among applicants coming from pure CS. Being a young professional during a time of unprecedented change puts me in a unique position to produce research that matters, and for me, Anthropic is the clearest path to achieving this goal.
