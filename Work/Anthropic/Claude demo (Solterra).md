## 1. Hook (5 min)

- Ask the audience: "How many of you have tried an AI tool before?"
- Follow-up: "What did you expect? What surprised you?"
- Brief personal intro: who you are, your connection to Anthropic/Claude

---

## 2. What Is Claude? (10 min)

- What is Anthropic, and how is it different from other AI companies?
- What is Claude? (AI assistant — not a search engine, not a chatbot in the old sense)
- What makes Claude different: thoughtful, safe, nuanced
- Quick overview of where/how people use it: work, home, school, creative projects

---

## 3. Live Demos — Claude in Everyday Life (20–25 min)

Each demo: show the prompt, show the response, narrate what's happening

- **Demo 1 — Writing help:** Draft a tricky email (e.g., asking a landlord to fix something, or a thank-you note)
- **Demo 2 — Understanding complex docs:** Paste in a confusing medical or legal paragraph, ask Claude to explain it in plain English
- **Demo 3 — Research & planning:** Plan a weekend trip, brainstorm a birthday party, or research a local topic
- **Demo 4 — Learning something new:** Ask Claude to explain a concept (e.g., how a mortgage works, or what inflation actually means)
- **Demo 5 — Creativity:** Ask Claude to create a game via Artifacts

---

## 4. The Nerd Corner — Claude Code (5–10 min)

- Frame it: "This part is for the developers in the room — everyone else gets a peek behind the curtain"
- What is Claude Code? (AI that lives in your terminal and helps you write, review, and understand code)
- Quick demo: ask Claude Code to write a simple script or explain a block of code
- Why it matters: it's changing how developers work day-to-day

---

## 5. Big Picture — Where Is This All Going? (5 min)

- What AI is good at vs. where it still struggles (honesty builds trust with the audience)
- How to think about AI as a tool, not a replacement
- Anthropic's approach to safety and responsible AI

---

## 6. Q&A (15–20 min)

- Open the floor

    - "What's something you'd want to use this for?"
    - "What worries you about AI?"
    - "Has anyone tried Claude Code or a similar tool?"
