## Overview of Generative AI
**Traditional AI vs Generative AI**
- Generative AI can create new content
	- Example: writing new emails 
- Traditional AI can analyze and categorize existing data
	- Example: classifying spam emails
- Development of generative AI represents a huge step in AI science

**Large Language Models (LLMs)**
- **Definition**
	- LLMs are trained to understand and produce human language
	- LLMs contain billions of parameters (values that determine outputs)
- **Pre-training**
	- LLMs analyze patterns across textual data to understand statistical relationships within language
	- LLMs build complex maps of language to make accurate predictions
- **Fine-tuning**
	- LLMs learn to follow instructions based on human feedback
	- This stage focuses on effective and responsible communication between AI agents and humans 

**Pillars of Generative AI**
- **Algorithms:** i.e., neural networks, transformers
	- Transformer architecture was developed in 2017
	- Transformers are excellent at processing sequences of text while retaining language structure
- **Data:** i.e., articles, websites, code, multimodal content
	- Large pool of data is critical for creating a state of the art AI agent
- **Computation:** i.e., TPUs, GPUs, computing clusters

**Size vs power**
- **Scaling laws**
	- The growth of models, their computing power, and their training data follows a predictable pattern
- **AI context window**
	- Practical limit to how much information an AI agent can consider for each prompt
	- This usually includes the prompt itself, the AI agent’s responses, and additional information from the human

**Three key aspects of generative AI**
- Processing vast information during training and learning complex language patterns 
- Adapting to new tasks via contextual learning without requiring additional training
- Demonstrating scaled capabilities 

## Pros and Cons of Generative AI
**Capabilities**
- Advanced language skills
- Multitasking 
- Vast variety of abilities
- Connection to other applications

**Limitations**
- Bounded by timing of training (knowledge cutoff date)
- Lack of verification of information (hallucination)
	- Output can be vastly wrong without the AI (or you) being aware
- Limited memory capacity (AI context window)
	- Information can be deleted as the conversation progresses
- Randomness or lack of consistency of outputs (temperature)
- Limitations in complex reasoning, particularly mathematical reasoning
	- Progress is being made

**Progress**
- Retrieval augmented generation: training technique that connects models external data to expand their reasoning capabilities

## Delegation
**Functions**
- **Problem awareness:** problem solving and task management with AI
	- To delegate well, you have to understand the task at hand and effectively break it down into actionable components
	- You also have to understand which aspects of the task will be best done by AI and which will be best done by humans
	- Problem solving is based on an understanding of your own success, work ethic, and expertise 
- **Platform awareness:** understanding capabilities of different AI systems
	- There is no one perfect system, but there could be one that is best for the task at hand
	- For example, Gemini is better with math and physics, while ChatGPT is excellent at writing, and Claude is the best with coding
- **Task delegation:** dividing work between AI and humans
	- Knowing which parts of the workflow could be automated
	- Choosing the approach to interact with AI agents
		- i.e., augmentation approach vs working separately 

## Description
**Functions**
- **Product description:** effectively crafting prompts for best output
	- Fully explaining the task, asking questions, correcting the output when necessary
	- Specifying context, format, style, etc
	- Give the AI agent the necessary information to produce the output
		- **Prompting tips:** provide specific context, offer examples, explain output constraints, break down complex tasks, give the AI space, define roles
- **Process description:** guiding an AI agent’s thought process
	- Guiding how the AI agent approaches the problems 
	- Providing relevant training for the task at hand
- **Performance description:** defining the behavioral aspects of AI interaction
	- Understanding that AI agents are interactive and behave differently in different situations 
		- They should not be used as databases
	- Sometimes telling the AI agent how to work is necessary for good output

## Discernment
**Functions**
- **Product discernment:** ability to judge the quality of AI output
	- Carefully reviewing AI generated content and judging its quality and accuracy
- **Process discernment:** ability to judge the quality of the AI’s thought process
	- Checking for inconsistencies, unnecessary steps, circular reasoning, inefficiency, etc
- **Performance discernment:** ability to judge AI behaviors
	- Reviewing communication style, information level, response to feedback, interaction, etc
- **Feedback discernment:** effectively providing feedback to AI output
	- Clearly specifying the problem, providing constructive criticism, revising original prompt

## Diligence
**Functions**
 - **Creation diligence:** critically choosing AI agents to work with
	 - Be aware of data ownership, training processes, privacy, etc
	 - Check policies before sharing sensitive information
 - **Transparency diligence:** ability to be open about stakeholders
	 - Communication should be transparent and effective
	 - When using AI on deliverables, be upfront about doing so
 - **Deployment diligence:** ability to take responsibility for AI outputs
	 - Checking accuracy, usage rights, etc
