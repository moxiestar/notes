## Ideas
**Broad ideas**
Players start with a placement quiz, placing them in either a beginner, intermediate, or advanced level. Each player has their own sandbox filesystem within the game, first starting with basic applications and files (AI chat, documentation, essential files), and then building to a complex built-in terminal interface and fully-fledged projects. Exercises will involve building prompts with Scratch-style blocks, taking care of an adaptive AI pet, debugging code through the terminal. I have some of this built already, but not all of it. Feel free to ask refining questions. 

**Final summary**
- Product: a browser-native AI coding game with placement-based leveling, sandbox filesystems, guided prompt-building, terminal debugging, and an adaptive AI pet
- ICP: parents as the buyer, middle/high school students as the user
- Mission: help students build projects with AI without becoming dependent on it, while teaching how LLMs actually work
- Business model: one-time purchase for the full curriculum plus certificate, with $200 as the current target
- Go-to-market: direct personal outreach first, starting from a gifted-student listserv, with social ads as a later channel
