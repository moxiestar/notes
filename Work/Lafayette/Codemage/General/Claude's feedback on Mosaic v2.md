**1. Broaden (or sharpen) the audience** CodeMage targets high schoolers in the NC Triangle area. You could go bigger — adult learners, bootcamp graduates pivoting to AI, or enterprise upskilling. Or go deeper into a different niche (e.g., non-technical professionals learning to use AI for their specific field).

**2. Build a real progression system** There's no visible leveling, badges, streaks, or social proof of skill growth. Codecademy and Duolingo have shown that gamification drives retention. A proper XP system, leaderboards, and shareable certificates would significantly improve daily active engagement.

**3. Community & cohorts** CodeMage appears to be a solo product with no community layer. Adding Discord-linked cohorts, peer code reviews, or group challenges would make it stickier and create word-of-mouth growth.

**4. Breadth of tools** The curriculum is Claude/Claude Code-specific. You could teach a tool-agnostic AI fluency curriculum (covering GPT, Gemini, local models, etc.) — or go the opposite direction and build deep partnerships with AI tool companies to become _the_ official learning path for their platforms.

**5. Institutional / B2B play** There's a vague mention of "school licensing inquiries," but no real B2B offering. Selling to schools, coding bootcamps, or companies as an upskilling platform is a much higher-revenue path than $12/month subscriptions. Codecademy's business model shifted heavily toward B2B (Codecademy for Business) for exactly this reason.

**6. Adaptive learning** Right now it's a fixed curriculum. Adding AI-powered personalization — where the difficulty and pacing adapts to each learner — would be a meaningful technical differentiator.

**7. Project portfolio output** Learners should graduate with a portfolio of real shipped projects they can show employers. CodeMage mentions "build and ship" but it's unclear if there's any showcase or portfolio feature.

**8. Mobile experience** A three-panel coding interface doesn't work on mobile. Designing learning moments that work on phones (concept reviews, quizzes, flashcards for prompt patterns) extends your reach significantly.
