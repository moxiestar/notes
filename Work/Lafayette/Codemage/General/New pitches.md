## Pitches
**Original DYS listserv pitch**
I've spent the last few months working on CodeMage, an AI literacy platform for high schoolers. As a young developer coming from a soft science background, I had to teach myself how to code with AI. My university had plenty of coding courses, but there were few resources focused on AI. Now, as the models are getting more powerful and the job market is getting more competitive, it’s more critical than ever to learn how to work with AI. So I built a curriculum that teaches students to do just that. 
With CodeMage, students work through 12 levels that take them from writing their first prompt in a browser all the way to building real projects in the terminal with AI. There's a Socratic AI tutor called the Familiar that guides without giving answers, a full Codex of reference material, and a verified AI Literacy Certificate on completion.
If you know a high schooler who'd benefit, or you’re interested for yourself, try it out here ([www.codemage.dev](http://www.codemage.dev/ "Protected by Outlook: http://www.codemage.dev/. Click or tap to follow the link.")). I'm giving the first 25 users free passes for all of CodeMage - if you're interested, send me a message ([https://www.codemage.dev/contact](https://www.codemage.dev/contact)) and I'll send you a code. I’d love to hear your feedback.

## Tagline ideas
- AI education for the next generation
- Learn to create, not just consume
- Be a builder, not a user
- For curious kids who want more than what school gives them
- A browser-native game for curious middle and high school students who want to create with AI — not just consume it. Learn to code, debug, and build independently while discovering how to use AI responsibly and think critically about its limits
- An immersive browser-native game that helps middle and high school students build real projects, develop coding confidence, and understand how to use AI responsibly — all in one personalized environment
- Designed for curious learners who want more than worksheets, tutorials, or generic AI tools
- Players begin with a placement quiz, then move through a personalized path based on their level. Inside their own sandbox environment, they learn by:
	- building prompts with Scratch-style blocks
	- exploring and managing files in a real filesystem
	- debugging code through a terminal
	- caring for an adaptive AI pet
	- completing projects that grow in complexity over time

I built Mosaic as a student at UNC after noticing something: schools teach kids how to code by hand, but nobody teaches them how to work with AI. So I made a curriculum that does. Mosaic is a browser-native, AI-powered game for curious middle and high school students who want to create and think critically with AI, not just consume it. 

AI education for the next generation, for curious kids who want more than what school gives them.
