### Sustained response to symmetry in extrastriate areas after stimulus offset
- **Area:** Focus on SPN as opposed to SPCN - may be an interesting perspective
- **Synopsis:** Study tested the length and characteristics of the sustained posterior negativity (SPN) response to symmetrical visual stimuli
- **Hypotheses:** Responses to symmetrical visual stimuli are more negative than responses to asymmetrical stimuli; responses to symmetry occur because of the stimulus itself, independent of the task
	- Note: SPN is generated by the extrastriate symmetry network (see citation of Jacobsen and Höfel, page 2)
- **Methods:**
	- **Participants:** $n = 40$ 
		- 20 for the shape task, 20 for the color task
	- **Main experiments:** 
		- **Shape matching task:** Experiment where sequential pairs of pixelated squares appeared on a grey background, and participants had to indicate whether they were equally symmetric or not (either both symmetrical, both asymmetrical, or one of each)
			- Only 25% of the trials had the squares change in symmetry (from symmetrical to asymmetrical or vice versa)
		- **Color task:** Experiment where participants had to distinguish the color of pixelated squares (either blue or grey) that appeared on a grey background
			- 25 extra trials where both squares were blue were added, but were excluded from the final analysis
- **EEG:** 
	- Electrodes of interest: PO7, PO8
- **Statistics:** 
	- Mixed ANOVA between symmetry and experiment showed greater negativity for symmetrical stimuli (p < 0.001) in the first square of a given trial
	- Mixed ANOVA between symmetry of stimuli (first vs. second) and experiment showed greater negativity to symmetrical stimuli (p < 0.001)
	- Main effect of symmetry (p < 0.001)
	- No main effect of task
- **Key findings:** 
	- Hypotheses were confirmed - greater negativity was seen in response to the symmetrical stimuli, and the SPN responses lasted roughly 1000ms
	- Sensitivity to symmetry occurred regardless of task - can they really confirm this?
	- Length of SPN likely reflects the underlying cognitive processes, not the duration of the task - again, how can they confirm this?
	- The bilateral SPN, which arises in the extrastriate cortex, overlaps with the contralateral SPCN, which arises in either left or right occipital areas
		- They are distinctly different components, but because of the interplay between memory and perception, it may be worthwhile to study the two components together 
- **Citation:** Bertamini, M., Rampone, G., Oulton, J., Tatlidil, S., & Makin, A. D. J. (2019). Sustained response to symmetry in extrastriate areas after stimulus offset: An EEG study. *Scientific Reports, 9*(1), 4401. [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-40580-z](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-40580-z)

### Dynamics of attentional allocation to targets and distractors during visual search
- **Area:** Effects of distractors on N1pc/N2pc and PD related to an electrophysiological measure
- **Synopsis:** Researchers measured attention with both the N2pc and PD, as well as the steady-state visual evoked potentials (SSVEP), an electrophysiological measure that lessened in the presence of distractors
- **Hypotheses:** SSVEP should be reduced and PD should be amplified in the presence of distractors 
- **Methods:** 
	- **Participants:** $n = 86$ before exclusion
		- 34 in Experiment 1, 21 in Experiment 2, and 23 in Experiment 3 (numbers included in EEG analysis)
	- **Main experiments:** Participants completed a simple search task while ignoring a distractor
		- **Experiment 1:** Four grey circles appeared in a diamond formation on a black background. Each circle flickered at a different frequency and changed color (green) at random intervals. Participants searched for a target (green diamond) and ignored a distractor (red square)
		- **Experiment 2:** Four grey circles appeared in a diamond formation on a black background. Each circle changed color (green) at random intervals. Participants searched for a target (green diamond) and ignored a distractor (red square)
		- **Experiment 3:** Four green circles appeared in a diamond formation on a black background. Participants searched for a target (green diamond) and ignored a distractor (red square), which both appeared at random intervals
- **EEG:** 
	- Electrodes of interest: P7, PO7, P8 PO8, P9, P10
- **Statistics:** 
	- Experiment 1/2: Greater PD was elicited by lateral distractors than lateral targets (p < 0.0001), and amplified when distractors appeared alone (p = 0.01)
	- Experiment 1/2: Greater N1pc was elicited by lateral targets than lateral distractors (p < 0.0001), and amplified when targets appeared alone (p < 0.01)
	- Experiment 3: N1pc did not appear with distractors, but a small N1pc appeared with targets
	- Experiment 3: N2pc appeared with lateral targets (p < 0.0001)
- **Key findings:** 
	- Cues (present in Experiments 1 and 2) elicited a more negative N1pc 
	- Largely, the PD appeared with distractors and the N1pc appeared with targets
	- Whether or not the cues flickered had little effect on the visual search processes, but removing the cues altogether elicited an N2pc instead of an N1pc
- **Citation:** Forschack, N., Gundlach, C., Hillyard, S., & Müller, M. M. (2022). Dynamics of attentional allocation to targets and distractors during visual search. *NeuroImage, 264*, 119759. [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2022.119759](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2022.119759)

### Electrophysiological and behavioral evidence for the attention capture and suppression failure of irrelevant singleton in test anxiety 
- **Area:** Focus on N2pc, SPCN, & PD related to anxiety and attentional biases
- **Synopsis:** Study measured the difference in responses to single distractors in groups with high test anxiety (HTA) and low test anxiety (LTA)
- **Hypotheses:** 
	- The HTA group will show a stronger response to and poorer performance after distractor presentation
	- The LTA group will be able to ignore the distractor and will show a larger PD
- **Methods:**
	- **Participants:** $n = 56$
		- Participants were grouped based on the test anxiety scale:
			- HTA: 20 or higher
			- LTA: 12 or lower
		- 28 participants in the HTA group, 28 participants in the LTA group
	- **Main experiments:**
		- **Additional singleton task:** Participants completed a search task while ignoring a single distractor
			- Nine circles (distractors) and one diamond (target), all containing a horizontal or vertical line, appeared on a black background 
			- One circle, the single distractor, was a different color
			- Stimuli were either green or red: if the main distractors and target were green, the single distractor was red
			- Participants responded based on the orientation of the line inside the stimuli
- **EEG:**
	- Electrodes of interest: P7, P8
	- This location captured all three key components (N2pc, SPCN, PD)
- **Statistics:**
	- RM ANOVA between condition and group showed an effect of group on accuracy (p = 0.013)
	- RM ANOVA showed effect of condition on response times (p < 0.001)
- **Key findings:**
	- Presence of the distractor impeded accuracy and speed
	- Response time differed more greatly with the presence of the distractor in the HTA group than in the LTA group
	- Accuracy was higher for the HTA group than for the LTA group
	- HTA group showed a larger N2pc and SPCN, while the LTA group showed a larger PD
	- HTA group showed an N2pc only when the target appeared, while the LTA group showed an N2pc both when the target and the single distractor appeared
	- Both the HTA and LTA showed an SPCN when the target appeared
- **Citation:** Hu, C., Song, J., Hong, Y., & Zhou, R. (2023). Electrophysiological and behavioral evidence for the attention capture and suppression failure of irrelevant singleton in test anxiety. *Journal of Psychiatric Research, 161*, 386–392. [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpsychires.2023.03.044](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpsychires.2023.03.044)

### Sustained posterior contralateral activity indicates re-entrant target processing in visual change detection
- **Area:** Focus on N1pc, N2pc, and SPCN related to luminance and laterality
- **Synopsis:** Length of SPCN elicited after N2pc directly correlated with response times, suggesting that SPCN is related to reprocessing information 
- **Hypotheses:** N1pc should appear with lateral luminance changes; N2pc should appear with selective attentional processes; SPCN should appear with more complex discriminational attentional processes, and its length should be correlated with 1) the length of the change localization processes and 2) response times
- **Methods:** 
	- **Participants:** $n = 12$
	- **Main experiments:** Participants responded to changes in the luminance and orientation of two lateral bars. Bars would appear either horizontally or vertically, either black or white, on a grey background. After the initial stimulus presentation, the bars would reappear, and participants would respond based on the changes - either in luminance, orientation, or both. 
- **EEG:** 
	- Electrodes of interest: PO7, PO8
- **Key findings:** 
	- Change-related positivity was seen in all four experiment conditions
	- N1pc was seen in all conditions, but its amplitude varied 
	- N2pc and SPCN were seen in three out of four conditions
	- SPCN was greater in conditions that required more intensive working memory processing  
- **Citation:** Schneider, D., Hoffmann, S., & Wascher, E. (2014). Sustained posterior contralateral activity indicates re-entrant target processing in visual change detection: An EEG study. *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience, 8*. [https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2014.00247](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2014.00247)

### Automatic Guidance (and Misguidance) of Visuospatial Attention by Acquired Scene Memory: Evidence From an N1pc Polarity Reversal
- **Area:** Focus on visual search, contextual cueing, N1pc, N2pc (PCN here), and SPCN (CDA here)
- **Synopsis:** Repeating visual search tasks leads to visual learning, reflected in the N1pc; when tasks are modified, a positive component is seen instead of a negative one
- **Hypotheses:** Contralateral positivity should occur after target presentation in expected location; SPCN should occur following initial negativity, reflecting ongoing attentional/working memory processes
- **Methods:** 
	- **Participants:** $n = 16$
	- **Main experiments:** Participants focused on a horizontal T (rotated either left or right) among an array of white corner shapes on a grey background. The T shape would be swapped with a distractor, and participants would identify it and respond based on its orientation 
- **EEG:** 
	- PO7, PO8
- **Statistics:** 
	- Significant main effect of context (p < 0.001) where context refers to whether or not the stimulus configuration has been repeated
	- Significant main effect of phase (p = 0.026) where phase is the stage of repeats, either before repeats or after
	- Significant main effect of epoch (p < 0.001) 
	- Significant interaction effect between context and phase (p < 0.001)
- **Key findings:** 
	- Shorter response times and higher accuracy in the repeat phase indicated successful spatial learning
	- N1pc was negative in the initial phase and positive in the repeat phase
	- N2pc was more negative in the initial phase than in the repeat phase 
	- SPCN was strongly negative in the initial phase, but did not occur in the repeat phase
- **Citation:** Zinchenko, A., Conci, M., Töllner, T., Müller, H. J., & Geyer, T. (2020). Automatic Guidance (and Misguidance) of Visuospatial Attention by Acquired Scene Memory: Evidence From an N1pc Polarity Reversal. *Psychological Science*. [https://doi.org/10.1177/0956797620954815](https://doi.org/10.1177/0956797620954815)
