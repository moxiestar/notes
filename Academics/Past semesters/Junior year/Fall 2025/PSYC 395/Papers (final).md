### I focus only when I see your fear—Fearful faces are not prioritized by attention when processed outside of awareness
- **Background:**
	- Full list of components: P1, N170, P2, N2, EPN, N2pc, P3, SPCN
	- **N2pc:** The N2pc is a negative ERP component that typically appears around 200-300ms after stimulation. It appears in the parietal and occipital areas of the brain, always contralateral to the visual stimulus. In electroencephalographic recordings, it is measured by the PO7 and PO8 electrodes. Stimuli in the lower visual field elicit a significantly stronger N2pc than stimuli in the upper visual field
	- **SPCN:** The sustained posterior contralateral negativity (SPCN), or CDA, is a negative deflection observed in the parietal and occipital areas. It appears at around 400ms in the hemisphere contralateral to the stimulus. In EEG recordings, it is strongest around electrodes PO7 and PO8. The SPCN is commonly measured in research on visual working memory. Notably, its amplitude is positively correlated with the number of visual stimuli (Chen 2022)
	- **P3:** One of the oldest ERP components, the P3 was named in 1965. During experiments where unpredictable stimuli appeared, those stimuli elicited a positive frontal spike at roughly 300ms. It is one of the most well-researched and popular components in cognitive psychology (Luck 2014)
	- **N170:** The N170 is an ERP component that appears as a negative deflection roughly 150-200ms after stimulus presentation. The component occurs laterally and is stronger on the right side of the head. It is generally agreed to be elicited by faces, though some scientists believed it is correlated with expertise (Luck 2014)
- **Hypotheses:** 
	- Threatening stimuli have greater attentional salience
	- P1 is equal across facial stimulus conditions
	- N170 differs across facial stimulus conditions
	- Fearful faces elicit greater late negative components
- **Methods:** 
	- **Participants:** $n = 41$ 
	- **Main experiments:** 
		- **Main structure:** 
			- Fixation (1000-2000ms jitter)
			- Faces (16ms)
			- Masks (pixelated faces) or fixation (50ms)
			- ISI (500ms)
			- Asterisks (150ms)
			- Response (keypress until 5000ms)
		- **Facial expression identification task:** Faces appeared on either side of a fixation cross for 16 ms and were then followed by either masks or blank fixation, then by two asterisks, either horizontally or vertically stacked. Participants had to indicate the facial expression that appeared on the same side as the asterisks
		- **Dot probe task:** Faces appeared on either side of a fixation cross for 16 ms and were then followed by either masks or blank fixation, then by two asterisks, either horizontally or vertically stacked. Participants had to indicate the orientation of the asterisks
		- **Stimuli:** NimStim face database, some faces pixelated in the masked condition
- **EEG:** 
	- **Electrodes of interest:** 
		- **P1, N170, EPN:** P7, P8, PO7, PO8, P9, P10
		- **P2:** PO3, POz, PO4, O1, Oz, O2
		- **N2:** F1, F2, Fz, FC1, FC2, FCz
		- **P3:** CP1, CPz, CP2, P1, Pz, P2
		- **N2pc, SPCN:** P7, P8, PO7, PO8, P9, P10
	- **Time windows:**
		- P1: 80-120ms
		- N170: 150-190ms
		- P2: 200-250ms
		- N2: 200-280ms
		- EPN: 250-350ms
		- P3: 350-650ms
		- N2pc: 250-350ms
		- SPCN: 350-550ms
	- **Analysis:**
		- 10-20 system with 64 channels
		- Referenced to mastoid electrodes (“left and right earlobes”)
		- Band-pass filter of 0.1-40Hz
		- Epochs of 1400ms
		- ICA was performed to remove ocular artifacts
- **Statistics:** 
	- P1, N170, P2, EPN, N2, and P3 were tested against three levels: emotion (fearful or neutral), task relevance (relevant or irrelevant), and masking (masked or unmasked)
	- N2pc and SPCN were tested against three levels: emotion (fearful or neutral), task relevance (relevant or irrelevant), and side (ipsilateral or contralateral amplitude)
	- Behaviorally, participants showed a bias towards neutral faces in the masked condition
	- Significant interaction between trial type and masking in the dot-probe task (p = 0.022)
	- Significant main effect (ANOVA) of emotion on N170 - fearful faces elicited a stronger response (p < 0.001), particularly in the unmasked condition (p < 0.001)
	- Significant main effect (ANOVA) of masking on N170 - amplitude was greater with unmasked faces (p = 0.016)
	- Significant main effect of side (ANOVA) on N2pc (p < 0.001)
	- Significant interaction effect between side and masking (ANOVA) on N2pc (p < 0.001), but the main effect of side only appeared in the unmasked condition (p < 0.001) where contralateral amplitudes were lower than ipsilateral amplitudes
	- Significant interaction between side and masking (ANOVA) on SPCN (p = 0.008) - amplitude was more negative contralaterally in the unmasked condition (p = 0.003)
	- Significant interaction between side and task (ANOVA) on SPCN (p = 0.021) - amplitude was more negative contralaterally in the ID task (p = 0.007)
	- Significant main effect of masking (ANOVA) on the SPCN (p = 0.035) - amplitude was more negative after unmasked stimuli
- **Key findings:** 
	- N170 was spiked following all fearful faces
	- SPCN and N2pc were spiked only following cognizant viewing of fearful faces
	- Fearful faces seem to be processed automatically and quickly, evidenced across a wide variety of ERP components, but encoding is still done carefully and does not necessarily spark threat-response networks in the brain
	- Conscious perception of facial stimuli was reflected in the N2pc and SPCN
	- Closed and opened mouths in the neutral and fearful faces respectively may have been a limitation in terms of encoding emotion
	- The difference in task difficulty across the two experiments may have confounded the results
- **Questions:**
	- Is the number of components a confounding variable? 
	- Worth researching the effect of top-down processing on fear-based responses?
	- What is the overlap between attention and consciousness?
	- Why were the ERP effects not reflected in the behavioral results (accuracy, response time, etc)?
- **Citation:** Doradzińska, Ł., & Bola, M. (2023). I focus only when I see your fear—Fearful faces are not prioritized by attention when processed outside of awareness. _Cerebral Cortex_, _33_(15), 9233–9249. [https://doi.org/10.1093/cercor/bhad194](https://doi.org/10.1093/cercor/bhad194)

### EEG dynamics reveal a dissociation between storage and selective attention within working memory
- **Note:** Complicated mathematical models - not appropriate for main focus
- **Area:** SPCN in selective attention & working memory, tested with retrocues
- **Hypotheses:** 
	- More reliable retrocues should produce a greater SPCN (no difference in SPCN across reliability conditions will indicate separation of working memory and attention)
	- Contralateral SPCN will be elicited after uncued stimuli
- **Methods:**
	- **Participants:** $n = 30$
	- **Main experiments:** 
		- Three black bars appeared around a fixation dot at random rotations, followed by a retrocue (a quarter of the fixation dot colored either red or green). After ISI, a probe appears (a quarter of the fixation dot colored white), corresponding with the target bar. Participants respond by indicating the exact rotation of the target
			- In half the experiment, retrocues were 80% valid; in the other half, retrocues were 50% valid
	- Lots of statistics that I don’t understand in this paper
- **EEG:** 
	- 10-20 system with 64 channels (+ 6 externals)
	- Electrodes of interest: P7, P8, PO7, PO8, O1, O2
	- Band-pass filter from 0.01 Hz to 6 Hz
	- Epochs from -500 ms to 1500 ms
- **Statistics:** 
	- Main effect of validity on error (p < 0.001) which appeared more prominently during the 80% validity condition
	- Interaction effect between laterality and reliability of cues (p = 0.004)
- **Key findings:** 
	- More reliable cues held attention 
	- Stimuli that will be relevant later are actively retained in working memory
- **Citation:** Günseli, E., Fahrenfort, J. J., van Moorselaar, D., Daoultzis, K. C., Meeter, M., & Olivers, C. N. L. (2019). EEG dynamics reveal a dissociation between storage and selective attention within working memory. _Scientific Reports_, _9_(1), 13499. [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-49577-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-49577-0)

### The neural correlations of spatial attention and working memory deficits in adults with ADHD
- **Area:** Clinical (ADHD) focus on attention via SPCN and N2pc
- **Hypotheses:** 
	- N2pc will be smaller in adults with ADHD during WM tasks (reflecting attentional span)
	- SPCN will be smaller in adults with ADHD during WM tasks (reflecting working memory capacity)
	- SPCN and N2pc amplitudes will be correlated
- **Methods:**
	- **Participants:** $n = 59$
		- 29 ADHD participants
		- 30 control participants
	- **Main experiments:** Visual search array
		- **Low load:** 2 notched squares (either red or green) appeared on either side of a fixation cross. After ISI, a visual search array composed of 12 notched squares appeared (1 red, 1 green, 10 black). Participants indicated whether the target (either red or green) color appeared in the search array
		- **High load:** 4 notched squares (either red or green) appeared on either side of a fixation cross. After ISI, a visual search array composed of 12 notched squares appeared (1 red, 1 green, 10 black). Participants indicated whether the target (either red or green) color appeared in the search array
- **EEG:** 
	- 128 channels (HydroCel Geodesic Sensor Net)
	- Referenced to mastoid electrodes
	- Band pass filter of 0.5-40Hz
	- ICA performed to remove ocular artifacts
- **Statistics:** 
	- ADHD group was less accurate in the high-load condition (p = 0.008) but not the low-load condition
	- ADHD group showed a smaller N2pc than the control group across both WM loads
	- ADHD group showed a smaller SPCN than the control group across both WM loads
	- N2pc predicted lower accuracy in the ADHD group
- **Key findings:** 
	- ADHD group performed more poorly than the control group in terms of accuracy and time 
	- These deficits were reflected in the EEG data: the ADHD group had a smaller N2pc and SPCN, indicating lower capacity for attention and working memory
- **Citation:** Luo, X., Guo, J., Liu, L., Zhao, X., Li, D., Li, H., Zhao, Q., Wang, Y., Qian, Q., Wang, Y., Song, Y., & Sun, L. (2019). The neural correlations of spatial attention and working memory deficits in adults with ADHD. _NeuroImage: Clinical_, _22_, 101728. [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nicl.2019.101728](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nicl.2019.101728)

### Spatial attention shifting to fearful faces depends on visual awareness in attentional blink: An ERP study
- **Area:** Attention to fearful faces via N2pc and N170
- **Hypotheses:** 
	- Shorter stimulus presentation time will reduce N2pc amplitude
	- Correlation will appear between N2pc and visual awareness negativity (VAN)
	- GET IN THE VAN FOOL!
- **Methods:**
	- **Participants:** $n = 26$
	- **Main experiments:** AB task
		- **Stimuli:** 
			- T1 stimuli: happy faces with a red tint
			- Distractors: upside-down black-and-white neutral faces 
			- T2 stimuli: black-and-white fearful face and neutral face
		- **T1-report:** Within an RSVP stream, T1 stimuli appeared at position 3, then T2 stimuli at either position 4 or 8, interspersed with distractors. Participants indicated the gender of the T1 stimuli, rated the clarity of the T2 stimuli, and indicated the location of the fearful face
		- **T1-ignore:** Within an RSVP stream, T1 stimuli appeared at position 3, then T2 stimuli at either position 4 or 8, interspersed with distractors. Participants ignored the T1 stimuli, but rated the clarity of the T2 stimuli and indicated the location of the fearful face
- **EEG:** 
	- 10-20 system with 64 channels
	- Electrodes of interest: P7, P8, P9, P10, PO7, PO8
	- Referenced to CMS & DRL
	- Filtered from 0.1-30Hz
	- Notch filter of 50Hz
- **Statistics:** 
	- Main effect of lag (position of T2 stimuli) on accuracy in the gender task (p < 0.001) and the fearful face location task (p < 0.001)
	- Accuracy suffered in the T1-report condition (p < 0.001)
- **Key findings:** 
	- Negative deflections contralateral to fearful faces were larger than ipsilateral deflections
	- SPCN occurred only when T2 stimuli appeared at position 8 
- **Citation:** Qiu, Z., Becker, S. I., & Pegna, A. J. (2022). Spatial attention shifting to fearful faces depends on visual awareness in attentional blink: An ERP study. _Neuropsychologia_, _172_, 108283. [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuropsychologia.2022.108283](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuropsychologia.2022.108283)

### Attentional capture by fearful faces requires consciousness and is modulated by task-relevancy: A dot-probe EEG study
- **Area:** Dot probe task and N2pc
- **Hypotheses:** 
	- N2pc will only appear during aware viewings
	- Dots in frequent positions will be identified more reliably than those in infrequent positions
- **Methods:**
	- **Participants:** $n = 26$
	- **Main experiments:** Dot probe task
		- **Supraliminal:** After fixation, facial stimuli appear for 166ms, followed by scrambled faces lasting 16ms, then a dot on either side of fixation. Participants responded by indicating the location of the dot
		- **Subliminal:** After fixation, facial stimuli appear for 16ms, followed by scrambled faces lasting 166ms, then a dot on either side of fixation. Participants responded by indicating the location of the dot
		- **Face relevance:** In half the blocks, participants were also asked to indicate the location of the fearful face
- **EEG:** 
	- 10-20 system with 64 channels
	- Referenced to the average
	- Filtered from 0.1-30Hz
	- Notch filter of 50Hz
	- ICA performed to remove ocular artifacts
- **Statistics:** 
	- Very confusing
- **Key findings:** 
	- When participants had to localize both the dots and the fearful faces, performance suffered
	- Greater negativity was seen in ERP data with longer stimulus presentations (supraliminal condition)
	- Dots that served as valid retrocues (appearing in the same location as the faces) produced greater ERP amplitudes
	- Relevant fearful faces evoked an SPCN
	- Based on these findings, fearful faces do not capture or hold attention
- **Citation:** Qiu, Z., Jiang, J., Becker, S. I., & Pegna, A. J. (2023). Attentional capture by fearful faces requires consciousness and is modulated by task-relevancy: A dot-probe EEG study. _Frontiers in Neuroscience_, _17_. [https://doi.org/10.3389/fnins.2023.1152220](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnins.2023.1152220)
