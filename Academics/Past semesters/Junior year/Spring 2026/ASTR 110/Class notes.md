## Module 1A
**Telescope anatomy**
- **Mount:** base on which the telescope sits
	- Most important part of the telescope
- **Primary mirror:** first mirror that light contacts when entering the telescope
- **Secondary mirror:** second mirror that light contacts when entering the telescope
- **Light baffle:** tube that directs light towards the filter wheel and digital camera
- **Filter wheel:** wheel behind the telescope that rotates the light through various colored lenses to be processed by a digital camera

**Photo processing**
- Camera senses how much light comes through in different areas of the photo
- Individual RGB images are then layered to create a full-color image
- **Percentile technique:** assumes the brightest color in the image is white, and sets the brightness/color of the image accordingly
- We can also reference background stars to correctly judge brightness

## Textbook 11.2 
**Jupiter’s atmosphere** 
- **Features on Jupiter’s surface**
	- **Great Red Spot:** oval atmospheric blob (their words, not mine)
		- Observed for at least 300 years
		- At least two times larger than Earth
		- Could be caused by atmospheric motion
		- Color is likely caused by the storm’s size and strength
	- **Atmospheric bands**
		- Bright zones and dark belts that cover the planet
		- **Zones:** high pressure areas
		- **Belts:** low pressure areas
		- **Zonal flow:** directional movement between bands
- **Atmospheric conditions**
	- Most abundant gasses: molecular hydrogen and helium 
	- These have a significant effect on Jupiter’s gravity (stronger)
	- Jupiter has no seasons
- **Jupiter’s colors**
	- Source is unknown
	- May be due to chemical processes in the upper atmosphere

**Jupiter’s moons**
- Jupiter has 67(!!!) total moons
- **Galilean moons:** largest moons
	- **Four Galilean moons:** Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto
	- Their orbits are circular and occur near Jupiter’s equator
	- **Innermost moons:** Io and Europa
		- Both have rocky mantles and iron cores
		- Io has extremely hot volcanoes, a thin atmosphere, and the youngest surface of any planet in the solar system
			- Its energy source is Jupiter’s gravity
		- Europa’s surface is covered in ice, possibly hiding water beneath
			- Life on Europa??
	- **Outermost moons:** Ganymede and Callisto
		- Both are largely composed of water and ice
		- Ganymede, like Europa, is icy 
		- Callisto, like our moon, has more craters

**Saturn’s atmosphere**
- Compared to Jupiter, Saturn is smoother and less saturated
- **Atmospheric conditions**
	- Most abundant gasses: molecular hydrogen, helium, methane, ammonia
		- Less helium is present than on Jupiter
	- Gravity is weaker than Jupiter’s
		- As a result, the atmosphere is much thicker
	- **Weather:** stable east-west zonal flow on the surface
		- Fewer large storms on Saturn’s surface

**Saturn’s rings**
- Saturn’s rings are extremely thin (only 10-15 meters)
- **Structure**
	- **Seven rings:** A, B, C, D, E, F, G
	- **Two gaps:** Cassini Division and Encke Gap
- **Composition**
	- Rings are composed of many, tiny, independent particles 
	- These particles are mostly water ice
- **Roche limit:** distance between planet and moon where the moon is destroyed
	- Also known the **tidal stability limit**

**Saturn’s moons**
- Saturn has eight large moons, most made of ice and snow
- **Titan**
	- Saturn’s largest moon
	- Surface temperature is very cold
	- Methane pools may be present on the surface
	- **Atmosphere**
		- More substantial atmosphere than any other moon (thicker than Earth’s)
		- Mostly made up of nitrogen
		- Gasses in the atmosphere mix to create its trademark smog

**Uranus**
- **Atmosphere**
	- Most abundant gasses: molecular hydrogen, helium, methane
	- Extremely cold
	- Variable weather
- **Moons**
	- 27 moons in total(!!!)
	- Five largest moons are similar to Saturn’s biggest moons
		- Mostly covered in ice

**Neptune**
- **Atmosphere**
	- Most abundant gasses: molecular hydrogen, helium, methane
	- Extremely cold
	- Radiates heat from an unknown source (just like the guy I’m seeing)
- **Great Dark Spot:** largest storm on Neptune’s surface
	- Roughly the size of Earth
- **Moons**
	- 13 moons in total
		- Only 2 moons visible from Earth
		- None are regular(?)
		- Triton is the largest
			- Atmosphere is made up of nitrogen
			- Surface primarily consists of water ice
				- Notable lack of cratering
				- Possible earlier surface activity

## Module 1B
**Radio waves**
- Radio telescopes measure invisible radio waves
	- Like optical telescopes, radio telescopes are parabolic reflectors
	- Unlike optical telescopes, radio telescopes can be used during the day
	- Radio telescopes are good for measuring points that vary (i.e., pulsars)
		- They measure the target’s brightness with two oppositely polarized channels
- Equation for calculating a circular telescope’s angular resolution
	-  $𝜃 = \frac{1.22𝜆}{D}$ radians where 𝜆 is the light wavelength and $D$ is the telescope diameter
- **Interferometer:** multiple single-dish radio telescopes combined to create higher resolution images
- Measurements from a radio telescope’s raster map can be transformed into images
