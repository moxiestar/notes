| Cognitive categorization                                                             | Automatic cognitive grouping of <br>information in schemas                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Advantages of cognitive schemas                                                      | Ease of navigating environment,<br>increased amount of information,<br>increased speed of accessibility                                                                                                                          |
| Effect on decision-making                                                            | Can lead to biased/inaccurate<br>decisions made quickly                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Stimulus variability & complexity                                                    | Increased variability & complexity<br>within schemas has a variety of <br>benefits for cognition                                                                                                                                 |
| Social schemas                                                                       | Collection of cognitive schemas<br>related to social interactions                                                                                                                                                                |
| Accommodating vs. assimilating <br>social schemas                                    | People are typically much more likely<br>to assimilate (changing the stimulus)<br>than to accommodate (changing their schema)                                                                                                    |
| Schema activation                                                                    | Activation happens subconsciously, <br>often determined by cue salience and<br>schema accessibility                                                                                                                              |
| Dissociation model of stereotyping                                                   | Theory that when meeting new people,<br>we automatically activate our schemas, <br>then perform individuation                                                                                                                    |
| Implicit attitudes & bias                                                            | Subconscious beliefs that can shape our<br>perception of and behavior towards others                                                                                                                                             |
| Pager Hiring Managers Study                                                          | Study showed that white applicants, with<br>or without a prison record, are more <br>successful than black applicants, even<br>if the hiring managers were black                                                                 |
| Stereotype Threat                                                                    | Decrease in performance based on <br>stereotypes about our in-group                                                                                                                                                              |
| Steele & Aronson (1995) <br>academic ability threat study                            | Study where participants, black or white,<br>performed a diagnostic test - when black<br>participants were told it was diagnostic,<br>their performance worsened                                                                 |
| Canning et al (2019) mindset beliefs <br>and academic performance study              | Study measured student performance with<br>professors with fixed vs. growth mindsets <br>Fixed professors: white & Asian students<br>performed better<br>Growth professors: all groups performed better,<br>including minorities |
| Prejudice & discrimination                                                           |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Realistic conflict theory                                                            | Study showed that the perception of limited<br>resources was enough to induce conflict                                                                                                                                           |
| Social identity theory                                                               | Study showed that we perceive those in our<br>in-group as more positive and  <br>heterogenous and those in our out-group<br>as more negative and homogenous                                                                      |
| Amira, Wright, & Goya-Tocchetto (2019) <br>in-group love vs. out-group hate findings | Study found that though we default to helping<br>our in-group, in times of unrest, we will tend to<br>hurt our out-group                                                                                                         |
| Intergroup Contact Theory Factors                                                    | Equal status, common goals, intergroup<br>cooperation, support from authorities, <br>mutual interdependence                                                                                                                      |
