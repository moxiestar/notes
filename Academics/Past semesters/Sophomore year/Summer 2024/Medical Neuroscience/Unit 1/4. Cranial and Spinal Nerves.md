[Video 2](https://www.coursera.org/learn/medical-neuroscience/lecture/49Bxj/overview-of-the-spinal-nerves) **==Overview of the Spinal Nerves==**

**31 (??) spinal nerves:**
- 8 cervical nerves
- 12 thoracic nerves
- 5 lumbar nerves
- 5 sacral nerves
- 1 coccygeal nerve

**Spinal cord enlargement (expansion of neurons):**
- Two areas are enlarged: cervical and lumbosacral 
- Fibres spread out in the cauda equina
- Enlargement allows for increase in circuitry 

**Spinal nerves:**
- Nerves send signals out to nearby muscle tissue
- Mixed spinal nerves: axons run through spinal nerves, end of axon enters spinal cord
- *Nerves are mixed with both motor and sensory components*

**Sympathetic chain:**
- Chain of ganglia running parallel to spinal cord
- Control smooth muscle, cardiac muscle, glandular tissue

*It's all connected*

**12 cranial nerves:**
- I (olfactory nerve) runs through the olfactory bulb
- II (optic nerve) forms an X shape (optic chiasm) above the corpus callosum
	- Close to infundibulum (pituitary stalk)
- III (oculomotor nerve) forms two branches
- IV (trochlear nerve) exits on posterior of CNS, moves eye downward and inward
- V (trigeminal nerve) passes behind pons
- VI (abducens nerve) moves the eye, most medial of three nerves along pons and medulla
- VII (facial nerve) most complicated nerve
- VIII (vestibulocochlear nerve) related to balance and hearing
- IX (glossopharyngeal nerve) just below 8
- X (vagus nerve) just below 8, controls gut-brain relations
- XI (spinal accessory nerve) flanks upper cervical spinal cord
- XII (hypoglossal nerve) protrudes tongue - when doctors ask you to stick out your tongue they are confirming the function of this nerve
