[Video 1](https://www.coursera.org/learn/medical-neuroscience/lecture/KLCvP/lateral-surface-of-the-brain) **==Lateral Surface of the Brain==** 
_Learning Objectives:
Lateral Surface of the Cerebral Hemispheres
1. _Demonstrate the four paired lobes of the cerebral cortex and describe the boundaries of each._
2. _Sketch or craft using common objects the major features of each cerebral lobe, as seen from the lateral view, identifying major gyri and sulci that characterize each lobe._

**Notes on the lateral surface:**
- Frontal lobe is composed of the superior frontal gyrus, middle frontal gyrus, inferior frontal gyrus
- Central sulcus extends from dorsal midline to the lateral fissure. Everything to the front of that is the frontal lobe. 
- Behind central sulcus - parietal lobe. Composed of two superior and inferior parts. Deep sulcus at the centre of the parietal lobe, the "intraparietal" sulcus. 
- Behind parietal lobe - occipital lobe. Divided from parietal by the parieto-occipital sulcus (posterior boundary of precuneus)
- Beneath lateral fissure - temporal lobe. 
- Lateral fissure is longer in left hemisphere, contributing to language processing. 

[Video 2](https://www.coursera.org/learn/medical-neuroscience/lecture/smRvV/medial-surface-of-the-brain) **==Medial Surface of the Cerebral Hemispheres==**
_Learning Objectives:
1. _Demonstrate the four paired lobes of the cerebral cortex and describe the boundaries of each.
2. _Sketch or craft using common objects the major features of each cerebral lobe, as seen from the medial view, identifying major gyri and sulci that characterize each lobe._

**Notes on the medical surface:**
- Forebrain (prosencephalon)
	- Cerebral cortex (telencephalon)
	- Thalamus & hypothalamus (diencephalon)
- Hindbrain (rhombencephalon)
	- Brain stem
	- Cerebellum
- Middle (mesencephalon)
	- Pons and cerebellum (metencephalon)
	- Medulla oblongata (myelencephalon)

**Gyral structure:**
- Above diencephalon is the corpus callosum, cradled by the cingulate gyrus
	- Cingulate gyrus > cingulate sulcus > superior frontal gyrus
- Paracentral lobule (bounded by superior frontal gyrus and parietal lobe)
- Precuneus gyrus (boundary of parietal and occipital lobe)
- Calcarine sulcus (above the cuneus gyrus and lingual gyrus)

[Video 3](https://www.coursera.org/learn/medical-neuroscience/lecture/wJ4kL/finding-the-central-sulcus) **==Finding the Central Sulcus==**
_Learning Objectives:
1. _Recognize the central sulcus from its medial terminus in the paracentral lobule to its lateral terminus in the lateral fissure.
2. _Sketch the central sulcus in the cerebral hemisphere and label the segments of the pre- and post-central gyri that represent somatic motor control and somatic sensation for the contralateral leg, arm and face.

**Central Sulcus:**
- Long sulcus that starts in the paracentral lobule, makes an S bend, and ends at lateral fissure
- Bounded by the post (sensory) and precentral (motor) gyrus!
- Foolproof way to identify the central sulcus: 
	- Follow the cingulate gyrus up to the cingulate sulcus
	- Note where the cingulate sulcus takes a sharp upward turn
	- The sulcus to the left ? of that is the central sulcus
 
[Video 4](https://www.coursera.org/learn/medical-neuroscience/lecture/cQmQQ/ventral-surface-of-the-brain) **==Ventral Surface of the Cerebral Hemisphere==**
_Learning Objectives:
1. _Describe the major features of the cerebral lobes, as seen from the ventral view, discussing major gyri and sulci that characterize each lobe.
2. _Recognize the major embryological subdivisions of the brain that are visible from the ventral view._

**Notes on the ventral surface (from front to back):**
- Olfactory tracts - terminate in olfactory bulb - sense of smell
- Optic nerve - X shape (optic chiasm)
- Stalk of pituitary attaches to hypothalamus through the floor of the diencephalon
- Mammillary bodies - posterior limit of hypothalamus - bounded by cerebral peduncles
- Pons - connects brain stem to cerebellum
- Medulla oblongata - medullary pyramids (contain corticospinal tract, which contains motor cortex fibers). Adjacent to the "olive"

**Ventral surface of frontal lobe:**
- Orbital cortex - sits above the orbits in the skull 
- Towards the posterior - temporal lobe. 
	- Occipitotemporal gyrus on the inferior surface of temporal lobe
	- Parahippocampal gyrus - folds up into the hippocampus

[Video 5](https://www.coursera.org/learn/medical-neuroscience/lecture/ILX7D/building-a-digital-brain-fingers-to-gyri) **==Building a Digital Brain (Fingers to Gyri)==**
_Learning Objectives:
1. _Demonstrate using your fingers (in air or on a surrogate model of a human brain) the major gyri and sulci of each lobe within the cerebral hemisphere_

**Notes on visualizing the brain surface:**
- Surface is similar to fingerprints - unique but similar across many individuals

**Gyri in terms of fingers:**
- Central sulcus: bounded by two parallel gyri (post and precentral) - each a finger's width
	- These come together on the medial surface to form the paracentral lobule
- Frontal lobe: one finger each for the superior, middle, and inferior frontal gyri
- Parietal lobe: one finger each for superior and inferior parietal lobule
- Temporal lobe: one finger each for superior, middle, and inferior temporal gyri
- Ventral temporal lobe: one finger each for occipitotemporal and parahippocampal gyri
- Medial frontal lobe: one finger each for cingulate and superior frontal gyri
- Occipital lobe: one finger each for the cuneus and lingual gyri (calcarine sulcus between)
	- Precuneus gyrus in front of the cuneus
	- Parieto-occipital sulcus in between

[Video 6](https://www.coursera.org/learn/medical-neuroscience/lecture/p67m5/blood-supply-to-the-brain) **==Blood Supply to the Brain==**
_Learning Objectives:
1. _Identify the major blood vessels that comprise the anterior and posterior circulation of the brain.
2. _Discuss the source of blood to the anterior and posterior circulation.
3. _Sketch the anastomotic ring of blood vessels (the circle of Willis) at the base of the brain.
4. _Identify the major blood vessels that supply the spinal cord.
5. _Describe the system of vessels for venous drainage of blood from the brain into the jugular veins._

**Blood vessels in inferior surface of the brain (gross):**
- Blood is supplied to the brain from 2 major pairs of arteries (anterior: internal carotid arteries, posterior: vertebral arteries)
	- Posterior:
		- Basil artery (formed at the juncture of the vertebral and carotid arteries) which runs up towards the midbrain
			- Branches out to the superior cerebellar arteries - supply midbrain and pons
			- Splits into posterior cerebral arteries - supplies occipital and temporal
		- Posterior inferior cerebellar arteries - supply posterior inferior aspect of cerebellum 
		- Anterior inferior cerebellar arteries - supply cerebellum and brain stem
		- Vertebral and medullary arteries - supply spinal cord
	- Anterior: 
		- Posterior communicating arteries - connects internal carotid to posterior cerebral
		- Branches of carotid arteries 
			- Anterior cerebral artery - runs over optic chiasm; supplies cingulate, superior frontal, paracentral lobule
			- Middle cerebral artery - supplies temporal lobe, frontal lobe, lateral surface of brain
			- Posterior cerebral artery - supplies occipital lobe, inferior temporal 

**Veinous drainage of blood:**
- Dura mater (protective covering of brain surface)
	- Splits into venus sinuses that allow blood to drain from the brain
	- Superior sagittal sinus - channel running along sagittal split
	- Confluence of sinuses - sagittal sinus and minor sinus systems drain into this receptacle 
	- Transverse sinus becomes the sigmoid sinus - drains through the jugular 
	- Falx cerebri - forms separation between two hemispheres, small and narrow sinus that allows blood to drain from deep within the brain
