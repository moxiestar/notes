Cross section of spinal cord:
[![The spinal cord | Human Anatomy and Physiology Lab (BSB 141) | Study Guides](https://s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com/courses-images/wp-content/uploads/sites/102/2018/11/24220047/SpinalCordXSection.png)
**Vocabulary:**
- Dorsal, lateral, & ventral **column** - different locations of **white matter** in the spinal cord
- Dorsal & ventral **horn** - two areas of **grey matter** in spinal cord
- The horn is composed of cell layers called Rexed's Layers or Laminae
	- Dorsal takes I-VI, ventral takes VII-IX
	- Dorsal horn is sensory-based, ventral horn is motor-based
- Each area in the column has a different motor/sensory function
	- Dorsal is sensory (sensory axons ascend the spine)
	- Lateral is mixed motor & sensory 
	- Ventral is mixed motor & sensory (controls pain pathways)

[Video 7](https://www.coursera.org/learn/medical-neuroscience/lecture/Asi9i/internal-anatomy-of-the-spinal-cord-longitudinal-organization) **==Internal Anatomy of the Spinal Cord: Longitudinal Organization==**

**Breakdown of spinal cord:**
- More grey matter present in cervical (arms) and lumbosacral (legs)
- Grey matter reduced in thoracic 
- White matter decreases as we descend the spine (axons terminate along the spine)
- Lateral horn: lateral protrusion in thoracic (pre-ganglionic sympathetic neurons)
- More white matter in cervical than in lumbosacral
- Divisions in dorsal column - gracile & cuneate tract
- Antero-lateral system - sends pain and temperature signals to brain
- Lateral (more important) & ventral cortico-spinal tract - arms and hands
