2. _Discuss the circulation of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) in and around the CNS_

**Ventricles:**
- CSF is produced in ventricles and circulated around the brain
- Lateral ventricle - behind some kind of membrane. Largest ventricle. Extends into frontal and parietal lobe, all the way back to occipital
- Third ventricle - just below lateral. CSF passes through interventricular foramen (foramen of Monro)
- Fourth ventricle - between cerebellum and brainstem. Allows for draining of CSF, which then flows into arachnoid mater
	- Chronic alcoholism decays cerebellum

**Notes:**
- Striatum: caudate nucleus, nucleus accumbens, putamen
- Internal capsule (IC): fan-like system of white matter running through cerebral cortex and everything beneath it, passes through basal ganglia
- Globus pallidus: structure inside basal ganglia
- Medial of IC: thalamus & caudate nucleus
- Lateral of IC: putamen & globus pallidus
- Inferior of IC: nucleus accumbens
