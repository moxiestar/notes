**Modification of Neural Circuits in Early Life:**
- ACC - amplification, computation, and communication via thalamic circuits
	- Precritical phase: early phase of development that doesn't require visual experience
	- Critical phase: visual experience alters structure and function of circuits
- Visual field is mapped in the visual cortex in same way we see things 

**Ocular Dominance Columns:**
- Eyes send signals that are not combined at the level of the thalamus - they are only integrated in the cortical circuits
- Bias towards one eye or the other
- What are ocular dominance columns? Stripes of some kind in the visual cortex

**ODC:**
- More neurons are biased for both eyes, but some are still biased for each single eye
- Amblyopia - acquired blindness, inability to recognize the objects presented to one eye
- Experiments that closed one eye in a young animal showed that the closed eye could no longer process visual signals; however when these experiments were repeated in an adult animal, the closure of the eye did not affect visual processing
- This defined a critical period for the sensitivity of visual circuits

**Orientation and Direction Preference:**
- Orientation selectivity: neurons all share similar receptive fields across the visual field - they all share the same orientation preference 
- Direction selectivity: neurons process bars of light moving in the same direction
- Most neurons in the primary regional cortex exhibit orientation and direction preference 
- Pinwheels in visual cortex are organized with a density of pi

**Orientation and Direction Preference:**
- How do these preferences develop?
- Orientation preference is present at eye-opening
- Direction preference develops about a week later
- Raising animals in darkness deprives them of their direction selectivity 
- Direction-selective neurons do not have the ability to self-organize, and must be trained by visual experience with moving stimuli
	- Critical period for motion training is very brief
- Normal sensory motor experience has a profound effect on the formation and the maturation of neural circuits in the cerebral cortex
- Some properties (like direction selectivity) may not develop without normal experience at a critical period
- Abnormal sensory motor experience in critical periods can lead to lasting impairments

**Review of Hebb's Postulate:**
- Coordinated activity of a presynaptic terminal and postsynaptic cell would strengthen the synaptic connection between them
- Likewise, uncoordinated activity between synaptic partners 

**Role of Neurotrophins:**
- Production and release of neurotrophins is activity dependent
- Neurons that are connected and coordinated would strengthen their connections and be nourished by the release of neurotrophins 
	- Neurotrophins are important mediators of long lasting benefits of coordinated activity between pre- and postsynaptic neurons in developing circuits
- For circuits driven by abnormal experience (ie, those with poor coordination), they will favor LTD and eventually be eliminated since they are deprived of neurotrophic support
- In maturity, neurotrophins are an important mediator of plasticity as brain processes sensory motor experience 
	- BDNF is key for the recovering brain
	- Variations in BDNF gene make cortical circuits more or less adaptive 

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Whatever you do with patients with brain injury (that is, the sensory motor experience that your structure and reinforce as you interact with them) will modulate the production of neurotrophins and shape the plasticity of their recovering brains
