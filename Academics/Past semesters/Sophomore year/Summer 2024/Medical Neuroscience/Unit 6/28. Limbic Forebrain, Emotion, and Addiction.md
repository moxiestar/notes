**Emotion:**
- Pertains to implict modes of processing 
- Emotion - association between mind and body?
- Lange theory of emotion:
	- Emotion-provoking stimulus activates peripheral receptors
	- Smooth and striated muscle activities are then engaged
	- Peripheral responses are detected
	- Emotion elicted by peripheral feedback
	- Therefore, emotion is the consequence of physical feedback
- Criticisms of Lange theory:
	- Lange emphasized smooth & striated muscle effector systems in the experience of emotion (not just expression)
	- If so, why aren't people with spinal cord injuries emotionally impaired?
	- Contemporary critics question the obligatory interplay between brain and periphery

**Emotion as Associative Learning:**
- Emotions result from association of sensory stimuli with primary reinforcers
	- Sensory stimuli
		- Interoceptive (derived from within the body)
		- Exteroceptive (derived from outside the body)
	- Primary reinforcers
		- Rewards (anything for which we will work)
		- Punishers (anything that we will work to avoif)

**Cellular Mechanisms:**
- Amygdala is a key reciever of stimuli
- Convergence of different kinds of inputs on some structure - perfect for LTP

**Limbic System:**
- Located in medial frontal cortex
- Olfactory division 
	- Structures: olfactory bulb, olfactory cortex
	- Function: special sensory (olfaction, flavor)
- Parahippocampal division
	- Structures: hippocampus, posterior cingulate gyrus, thalamic anterior nucleus, posterior hypothalamus
	- Function: explicit processing (episodic memory acquisition and consolidation, spatial mapping)
- Amygdala/orbital prefrontal division 
	- Structures: amygdala, orbital medial prefrontal cortex, anterior hippocampus, anterior cingulate gyrus, temporal pole cortex, ventral striatum & pallidum, thalamic mediodorsal nucleus, medial hypothalamus
	- Functions: implicit processing (visceral motor control, emotional experience and expression, appetitive drives, social behavior)

**Amygdala:**
- Key for evaluating trustworthiness of another person
- Essentially an early warning system for the human brain 
- Plays a primary role in the association of sensory stimuli with reward and punishment
- Modulates information processing in the forebrain
	- Perceptual: enhances awareness of potentially dangerous stimuli
	- Mnemonic: enhanced memory for emotionally charged events
- Hypofunction produces deficits in the efficacy in information processing 
- Hyperfunction (hi) produces excessive vigilance and anxiety

**Orbital Medial Prefrontal Cortex:**
- Receives input from amygdala, as well as multi-modal sensory inputs from sensory and associational cortices
- Involved in emotional learning, interpretation of social cues, planning appropriate social behavior, formation of advantageous decisions
	- Emotional learning when rewards are scents or foods
- Involved in ongoing analysis and modification of behavior when reinforcement contingencies are rapidly changing (emotional relearning)
- Necessary for assessment of future consequences and implementation of advantageous decisions

**Injury to OMPC:**
- No impairments in sensory motor control (except for olfactory and taste impairments)
- No impairments on standard neuropsychological assessments
- Impaired emotional experience and expression
- Impaired rational decision making, especially in the domain of personal and social affairs
- Pathological inability to make advantageous or good decisions in real-life situations
- Judged with Iowa Gambling Test - very complicated but the upshot is that impaired patients will always choose cards that result in net losses (impulsive prediction of significant reward)
- Incapable of predicting long term consequences of their actions

**Somatic Marker Hypothesis:**
- Rational decision making entails a covert (subconscious) evaluation of future consequences
- Mental images representing possible outcomes trigger somatic states that mark the mental images with emotional significance 
- Somatic states may be truly somatic (involving activity of autonomic and skeleto-muscular effector systems) or vicarious (involving only neural representations of viscero- and somato-motor activity in parietal and insular cortex)
- Therefore somatic markes bias deliberations towards implementation of advantageous decisions

**Subjective Aspect of Emotion:**
- Feelings may be the consequence of having emotions within a cognitive faculty capable of self reflection
- Therefore feelings are a kind of working memory?
- Interaction of explicit hippocampal systems and implicit amygdala systems which are processed consciously in the prefrontal cortex
- Development of working memory is critical for emotion

**Summary:**
- Human emotion entails a wide range of brain and body states characterized by expressive somatic behavior, stereotypical physiological responses, and distinct subjective experiences
- These dynamic states arise as a result of associative learning that attaches emotional prevalence to sensory stimuli
- Associative emotional learning involves the amygdala and orbital medial prefrontal cortex
	- The amygdala is a primary neural center for associative learning
	- The orbitofrontal cortex also contributes, especially when contigencies of reinforcement are actively changing
