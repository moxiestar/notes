**Circadian Rhythms:**
- A variety of bodily functions follow daily rhythms, lasting approx. 24 hours
- Sleep may have something to do with thermal regulation 
- Circadian clock inside brain naturally operates over about 26 hours, but operates over 24 hours with cues
- Controlled by suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) in thalamus - trained to environment via projections from retina
	- Projections derived from photosensitive ganglion cells, which contain a melanopsin pigment 
	- Slow response to cone activity 
	- Monitor overall levels of light in environment and communicate that information to the SCN 
	- Even when extracted from the brain, the SCN cells continue to generate a rhythm

**SCN Mechanisms:**
- Two sets of pathways that feedback upon each other
- Two proteins (CLOCK and BMAL1) associate with each other and enter nucleus, forming a complex that regulates target genes, forming transcription factors that can activate target genes
- Another mechanism inhibits CLOCK and BMAL1 proteins through a negative feedback loop - very confusing, check unit guide

**EEG:**
- Means of recording brain activity or "waves" that generate macroscopic currents
- As neurons receive input, they're depolarized. Ions flow across the membrane, with positive charge entering post-synaptic cell
- This creates areas of source currents (positive) and current sinks (negative)
- This electrical dipolarization can be recorded by EEG
- EEG represents the sum of that electrical activity 
	- If the activity is more synchronized, the EEG is more clear (higher amplitude, lower frequency) and this is indicative of non-REM sleep
		- Highly synchronized EEGs are representative of seizures
	- However if the activity is irregular (lower amplitude, higher frequency), this is indicative of REM sleep
- Delta band (large amplitude, slow waves; 1-4 cycles per second; 1-4hz) is indicative of the deepest state of sleep
- Theta band (4-7hz) is indicative of wakefulness, engaged with surroundings
- Alpha band (8-13hz) is indicative of sensory cortex being disengaged from environment
- Beta band (above 12hz) and gamma band (40-60hz) are indicative of desynchronization in cortical networks

**Measuring Sleep with EEG:**
- Stage 1: Drowsiness - transition from vigilance to sleepiness. Emergance of higher amplitude rhythms
- Stage 2: Increase in amplitude, and emergence of sleep spindle (brief high frequency activity that occurs in clusters)
- Stage 3: Large increase in amplitude, lack of sleep spindle
- Stage 4: Greatest increase in amplitude, delta waves
- REM: Reversion to low amplitude, high frequency pattern

**REM Sleep:**
- Cortex is desynchronized from surroundings, yet certain areas of the brain (cingulate cortices, hippocampus, amygdala) have increased activity (explains the detailed and emotional nature of our dreams) while the prefrontal cortex has decreased activity (explains the socially inappropriate nature of our dreams)
- Rapid movements of eyes occur during REM sleep, as well as active suppression of limb movements
- Heart rate and respiration increase during REM sleep, though they subside in the other stages
- REM sleep may not be necessary for life - not everyone dreams

**Importance of Dreaming:**
- Freud: "ego" relaxes its hold on the "id"
- Dreams rehearse less common behaviors not often expressed in waking states (risky behaviors, aggression, etc)
- Dreams may erase unwanted memories
- Accepted belief: dreams may consolidate learning and memory
- Some believe REM sleep is important, and not dreams

**Neural Sleep Circuits:**
- Rhythms reflect a bi-stable condition of the thalamocortical projection neurons (TCPN)
	- TCPN exist in two states: oscillatory (correlates with deeper levels of non-REM sleep), or tonically active (allows for faithful transmission through thalamic regions)
		- During oscillation, the thalamus is essentially disconnected from the outside world, so that we're not distracted
			- However, a strong enough stimulus is enough to drive neurons out of oscillatory state, possibly even waking you up

**Notes:**
- If you don't wake up while the dream is happening, the dream will not be remembered 
- If can remember all of your dreams over a long period of time, you aren't sleeping well :-(

**Insomnia:**
- Inability to fall asleep, stay awake, or feel well-rested after sleep 
- Can be caused by caffeine and mitigated by benzos

**Circadian Rhythm Disorder:**
- Disruption of circadian rhythm
- Typically caused by lifestyle choices

**Sleep Apnea:**
- Cessation of breathing during sleep
- Often affects those who are obese
- Can be treated with a positive pressure mask to prevent pharynx from collapsing on airway

**Narcolepsy:**
- Allows for fragments of REM sleep to enter into waking state
- Patients aren't actually sleep - they're sort of half awake
- Cataplexy (partial or complete paralysis) can occur
- Attacks are often brought on by intense positive emotions, including laughter
- Mutation affecting orexin system

**Restless Leg Syndrome:**
- Movement in extremities, typically in lower legs, caused by a feeling of unease
- Associated with iron deficiency in dopamine neurons
