**Electrical signaling**
- Fundamental neuronal process underlying all aspects of brain function
- Neurons are intrinsically poor generators of electricity
- However neurons have evolved mechanisms to overcome their natural limitation
- Electrical signals are based on the flow of ions across the neuronal plasma membrane ?
- -40 to -90 millivolts inside neuron (resting membrane potential) - negative compared to the area outside the cell body
- Action potentials - active depolarization of the current inside the neuron
- Higher current leads to increased number of action potentials (several fired in a row)

**Key molecular mechanisms**
- Establishment of a concentration gradient of a permeant ion
- The flow of that ion down the concentration gradient by a means that allows for permeability through the plasma membrane
	- Ion gradients: active transports bind ions and transport them upstream
	- Ion channels: channel that allows ion to diffuse through aqueous pore in the centre of the channel, allowing for transport across plasma membrane
	- Ion pumps establish the concentration gradients that provide the driving force for diffusion of ions across the normal neuronal paths of the brain

**Na & K Channels:**
- At onset of depolarization, Na channels open, while K channels stay closed until later in the action potential
- Re-polarization delays the action potential
- Na channels swell when closed and shrink when open, allowing ions to flow through. When Na is inactivated, a small "gate" swings shut when membrane becomes depolarized and prevents further influx of Na channels (though the channel remains open). During this period we have the absolute refractory period. When the channel closes, the "gate" is reset open
- K channels open when Na is inactivated, and will remain as long as membrane is depolarized. However they have no "gate." When we return to resting membrane potential, K channels close, preventing further efflux of K channels
