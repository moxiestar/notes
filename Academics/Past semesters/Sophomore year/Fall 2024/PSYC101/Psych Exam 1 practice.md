	**Empiricism states that knowledge is based on experience. Rationalism states that knowledge is based on reason. When used in research, they balance each other out. The steps of research are: formulate a hypothesis, design the study, collect the data, analyze the data, report the findings. A theory is more broad, while a hypothesis is specific and testable. A hypothesis with an operational definition is a hypothesis that can be tested. A research study usually surveys people and doesn't necessarily include variables and control or experimental groups.** 
	**An extraneous variable can affect the dependent variable in your study, but it is not being measured. A confounding variable can affect both the dependent and independent variables, and it is also not being studied.** 
	**Types of research methods:**
		Naturalistic observation
		Case study
		Survey
		Experiment
	Quasi experiment does not rely on random assignment - used for intervention studies
		Typically experiments where you cannot actually test your hypothesis (ie, effects of cortical lesions) and have to go find people to perform case studies
		You can compare "control" and "experimental" groups but you did not actually create the conditions of the "experimental" group
		You cannot draw causal conclusions because there can be variables out of your control in a quasi-experiment
	Descriptive statistics: correlation
	Inferential statistics: causation
