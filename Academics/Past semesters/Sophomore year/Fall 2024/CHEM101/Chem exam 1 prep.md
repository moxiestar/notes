QUANTUM NUMBERS
Schrodinger: wave function - how the wave of an electron varies in time and space inside an atom
Wave function squared - probability of finding an electron within an atom - an orbital
N: principal quantum number - shell - indicates size and energy of orbital(s) in an atom.
L: angular momentum quantum number - number of orbitals (0 = s, 1 = p, 2 = d, 3 = f)
Ml: magnetic quantum number - orientation of orbitals
Ms: spin quantum number - direction of magnetic field ?

ELECTRON CONFIGURATIONS
Pauli exclusion principle: no two electrons can have the same set of quantum numbers (one must have Ms +1/2 and one must have Ms -1/2)
Aufbau principle: electrons are placed in the lowest-energy orbitals first
Hund's rule: lowest-energy configuration for degenerate orbitals is the one with the maximum number of valence electrons
Shells with n ≥ 2 have 3 p orbitals
Shells with n ≥ 3 have 5 d orbitals
Effective nuclear charge (Zeff): decrease in charge between subshell electrons and nucleus
Number of orbitals: n squared

BOHR MODEL
$E=(-2.178) 10^-18(1/n^2)$ - n = # of orbitals, e = energy
Ground state: an electron in the lowest energy level
Excited state: an electron in a level above n = 1
Graph: downward lines = emission, moving from a higher to lower level
Graph: upward lines = absorption, moving from a lower to higher level

IONIZATION ENERGY
Increases going down the periodic table (T to B)
Decreases going across the periodic table (L to R)
