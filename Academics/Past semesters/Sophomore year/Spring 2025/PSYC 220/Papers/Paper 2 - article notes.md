Expertise ([[Gauthier.pdf]])
- Hypotheses: the fusiform facial gyrus is specialized for stimuli of expertise and will show increased activity in response to such stimuli
- Methods: 
	- 19 participants: 11 car experts & 8 bird experts
	- Measures: fMRI analysis
	- Groups: 
		- Participants were shown images of faces, birds, cars, and objects that moved across a greyscale background
		- Participants were asked to identify either the location or the type of the stimulus as compared to the previous image
- Main findings: 
	- Birds produced greater FFA activity in all participants
	- Birds and cars produced an "expert" effect in the FFA
	- When compared to inverted stimuli, birds and cars produced strong FFA activity in bird and car experts respectively
	- FFA seems to be specialized for facial stimuli - results were unclear?
- Citation: Gauthier, I., Skudlarski, P., Gore, J. C., & Anderson, A. W. (2000). Expertise for cars and birds recruits brain areas involved in face recognition. *Nature neuroscience, 3*(2), 191-197.

Faces ([[Kanwisher.pdf]])
- Hypotheses: the fusiform facial gyrus is specialized for facial stimuli and will show increased activity in response to human faces
- Methods:
	- 15 participants
	- Measures: fMRI analysis
	- Groups:
		- Part 1: 5 participants were shown facial and object stimuli to detect an area of neural activity for facial stimuli
		- Part 2: 5 participants were shown a) faces and scrambled faces and b) faces and houses
		- Part 3: 5 participants were shown a) 3/4 faces and human hands 
- Main findings: 
	- Part 1 & 2: 5 participants showed increased FFA activity for unscrambled and frontal facial stimuli over other stimuli
	- Overall: 12/15 participants showed activity in FFA in response to facial stimuli over other stimuli
	- FFA is in fact specialized for facial stimuli over other types of stimuli
- Citation: Kanwisher, N., McDermott, J., & Chun, M. M. (1997). The fusiform face area: a module in human extrastriate cortex specialized for face perception. *Journal of neuroscience, 17*(11), 4302-4311.
